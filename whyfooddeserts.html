What Causes food deserts?

   According to this article by thenewshouse, "The USDA defines urban areas as food deserts when 500 people or 33 percent of the population lives more than one mile from a supermarket or large grocery store." which leads to whole communities not having access to nutritious food (newshouse article).    Slash and burn techniques contribute to carbon pollution in their relative areas due to the burning of large amounts of land producing thick and damaging smoke into the atmosphere which impacts on local populations of humans and wildlife. The smoke pollution caused by palm oil deforestation also impacts areas worldwide by significantly contributing to the global production of carbon dioxide and greenhouse gases. As noted in (Vijay et al., 2016)'s study on palm oil deforestation in tropical areas, the “annual carbon emissions from gross tropical deforestation” result in “nearly 10% of the global total of anthropogenic greenhouse gas emissions.” With palm oil deforestation detrimentally impacting local and worldwide communities, the issue of palm oil is highly important and requires consumers to take action in order to work towards reducing the production of palm oil.
